Every winter, the town of McCall, about 30 miles up Highway 55 from Cascade, throws one of the biggest parties in Idaho. The McCall Winter Carnival takes over the whole lakeside town for ten days with hand-carved snow sculptures the size of houses, live music, fireworks, and a Mardi Gras parade. If you've never been, it's the kind of thing you plan a trip around. Here's what to expect and how to do it right.

## What the Carnival Actually Is

Picture a mountain town that leans all the way into winter instead of hiding from it. For ten days, McCall's streets, storefronts, and parks fill with elaborate snow and ice sculptures built by local teams and businesses. Around them runs a full slate of events: a vendor court overlooking Payette Lake, live music, a torchlight ski parade, fireworks, a snowshoe scramble, and the big Mardi Gras parade that closes things out.

It started decades ago as a small community celebration and has grown into a signature Idaho event that draws visitors from across the region. The atmosphere is festive and family-friendly, with plenty happening for kids and grown-ups alike.

## When It Happens

The Carnival runs for ten days, bridging late January into early February each year. In 2027, for example, it runs January 28 through February 6, with the sculptures finished for judging by the end of that first week and the Mardi Gras Parade closing the festival on the final day.

Dates shift a little from year to year, so confirm the current schedule on the official [Visit McCall](https://visitmccall.org/events/winter-carnival/) site before you lock in your trip.

## The Snow Sculptures

The sculptures are the heart of it. Teams start days ahead, packing and carving enormous blocks of snow into dragons, castles, cartoon characters, and scenes you have to see to believe. Once they're judged, they stay up for the length of the Carnival, so you can wander the town on any day and take them in.

A few tips for the sculptures:

- **Go during daylight for photos,** but come back after dark, when many are lit up and look completely different at night
- **They're spread around town,** so plan to walk or shuttle between clusters
- **Weather is part of the show.** A fresh snowfall makes them magical; a warm spell softens the details, so earlier in the run is often crisper

## Beyond the Sculptures

Even if the carving weren't happening, the week is packed. Depending on the year's schedule you'll find a beer garden and vendor court, live bands, a fireworks show over the lake, snowshoe and ski events, kids' activities, and food from local restaurants. And you're in McCall, so world-class skiing at Brundage Mountain, groomed Nordic trails, and the frozen shoreline of Payette Lake are all right there between events.

## Why Stay in Cascade

Here's the local secret: McCall books solid and gets pricey during Carnival week. Cascade sits just 30 miles south on the same highway, a scenic half-hour drive, with fewer crowds and a calmer evening when the festival winds down. Stay at Birch Glen and you get the best of both: a full day up at the Carnival, then a quiet drive home to a warm room and our wood-fired sauna. Cascade also has its own winter to offer, from ice fishing on Lake Cascade to hundreds of miles of groomed snowmobile trails, so you can build a whole trip around the trip.

## Tips for a Smooth Day

- **Arrive early.** Parking in McCall fills up fast on Carnival weekends; get there before midday
- **Dress in real layers.** You'll be outside for hours, so waterproof boots, warm socks, hats, and gloves make or break the day
- **Bring cash** for vendors and parking
- **Check the parade route and road closures** the morning of, especially on the final Saturday
- **Watch the highway.** Winter driving on Highway 55 means snow and ice; leave early and give yourself extra time
